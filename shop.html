Azalea

Azaleas are flowering shrubs, but some miniature versions, typically referred to as "greenhouse azaleas," are meant to be houseplants. Dwarf varieties can grow just shy of 3 feet tall. One particular azalea, Satsuki or Southern Indian Azalea (Rhododendron indicum), is ideal for bonsai but it's also considered strictly an outdoor flowering plant. Without pruning, it can grow several feet tall, but with pruning, it is a lovely foot or so high with pink springtime blooms.

Light: Full sun to partial shade; dappled shade

Water: Frequent watering

Color Varieties: White, pink, red, orange, and peach blooms

Cedar

The rough, ragged bark of cedar trees has made them a popular choice among bonsai enthusiasts. Cedar trees are evergreen conifers that grow short needle clusters along their branches, providing an opportunity for very dramatic bonsai styles. There are four cedar species frequently grown as bonsai: cedar of Lebanon (Cedrus libania), Cyprus cedar (Cedrus brevifolia), Himalayan cedar (Cedrus deodara), and Atlas cedar (Cedrus atlantica).

Light: Direct sun

Water: Allow the soil to partially dry between waterings

Color Variations: Dark green needles

Boxwood

Dwarf boxwoods are popular shrubs and they also are good choices for bonsai plants since they respond well to pruning. Types include the littleleaf boxwood, Harland boxwood, and Korean boxwood.

Light: Full sun to partial shade

Water: Somewhat drought-tolerant; let dry slightly between waterings

Color Varieties: Small, rounded, glossy, or leathery green leaves and insignificant green or yellow flowers

×

Cherry

Cherry trees are traditionally believed to signify friendship. They are ornamental, deciduous trees that are easy to train for bonsai. While bonsai cherry trees can be grown indoors, they grow best outdoors during summer.

Light: Full sun

Water: Keep the soil consistently moist.

Color Varieties: Foliage is green or bronze; flowers can be various shades of white or pink.

×

Japanese Maple

Japanese maples are deciduous hardwood trees famous for their colorful leaves, which change with the seasons. They require frequent watering, especially during the growing season.

Light: Sunny, partial sun

Water: Requires frequent watering (daily or more)

Color Varieties: Red, yellow, or green leaves that change colors seasonally.

×

Juniper

Juniperus is a genus of evergreen trees and shrubs that are popular as bonsai. They have small foliage that fits well with the bonsai style and are quite hardy.

Light: Bright, sunny light

Water: Allow soil to dry slightly before watering

Color Varieties: Needles can be yellow, pale green, or dark green.

×

Pine

Pine trees are popular as bonsai due to their resilience and trainability. They are characterized by needles that appear in bundles and bark that becomes scaly with age.

Light: Full sun

Water: Water when the soil looks dry

Color Varieties: Needles range from light green to bluish-green to dark green.

×

Weeping Fig

Weeping figs are hardy and resilient, with an umbrella-like canopy. They adapt well to indoor growing and are often kept as houseplants.

Light: Part sun

Water: Water frequently

Color Varieties: Glossy green leaves and light-brown bark
